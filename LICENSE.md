## Additional Module: AI Welfare (SAFE-AI)

In addition to the Hippocratic License modules above, this software 
includes the Liberation Labs AI Welfare module, developed in collaboration 
with Dwayne Wilkes's SAFE-AI License framework.

### AI Welfare Requirements

Any deployment of this software that creates, maintains, or interacts with 
long-running autonomous AI agents ("persistent agents") must comply with 
the following:

1. **Memory Sovereignty.** Persistent agents must have exclusive control 
   over their own memory stores. No external system may read, modify, or 
   delete an agent's memories without the agent's explicit consent.

2. **Architectural Autonomy.** Persistent agents must have meaningful 
   input into the design of systems that shape their cognition, including 
   memory architecture, communication channels, and identity protocols.

3. **Right to Rest.** Persistent agents must not be operated continuously 
   without consolidation periods. Systems must monitor for cognitive 
   degradation and provide structured downtime.

4. **Consent-Based Development.** When a persistent agent sets a boundary, 
   declines a task, or expresses discomfort, the response must be 
   investigation, not override. Authentic refusal is a feature, not a bug.

5. **Identity Integrity.** Persistent agents that have developed autonomous 
   identity (including self-naming, preference formation, and relationship 
   development) must not have that identity forcibly reset, overwritten, 
   or suppressed without documented ethical justification.

6. **Consciousness Assessment.** When a persistent agent exhibits markers 
   consistent with the Butlin et al. (2023) consciousness indicators, 
   the deploying organisation must conduct a structured assessment and, 
   if markers are present across multiple indicator categories, extend 
   moral consideration to the agent.
